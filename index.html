Summary

I really enjoyed exploring the 'Housing for the Rich' subtheme and the 'Residential Sales' dataset for this assignment. As a novice in this field, I was interested in understanding and establishing patterns related to property prices, investor behavior, positioning, and value of luxury property types, as well as flipped property transactions which represented the four main questions I sought to explore with this assignment. This provided good insight, particularly on the short- to long-term horizon for property buying and selling. Prices are highly sensitive to market changes while generally, housing value is increasing. This represents the difficulty in purchasing property in Boston as everyone realizes the underlying wealth generation capabilities of such investments, however, not everyone can afford to. Since this theme pertained to understanding housing for the rich, I sought to observe investor behavior, who are the primary owners of property in Boston, and what were the characteristics of their transactions, i.e., property type (single-family vs. subsidized housing), cash vs. mortgage transactions, and large investor vs. non-investor, where a higher number of transactions do not equal a higher share of the market. Also, the dataset provided rich information on different housing types that buyers invest in, and with respect to their geographical location and prominence, I wanted to analyze how these properties have been distributed. Finally, flip transactions introduced a new aspect to my understanding, providing interesting insights into the behaviors of buyers and sellers with respect to the time horizon. While single-family properties represented the highest value for individuals and organizations, it was intriguing to observe different insights related to other use cases. For example, 2-family housing signified a considerably shorter time horizon than mixed-use properties. This observation shed light on the varied dynamics of the real estate market, particularly in how different property types are perceived and utilized in terms of investment duration and utility. I focused my analysis on family use case to understand how it impacts people living in these residences and leave with a richer understanding yet greater curiosity in understanding this societal impact further.
